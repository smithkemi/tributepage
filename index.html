About Chadwick Boseman

  • He was born November 29, 1976 in Anderson, South Carolina
  • He graduated from T. L. Hanna High School in 1995 where he played on the basketball team.

    • In his junior year, he wrote his first play, Crossroads, and staged it at the school after a classmate was shot and killed.

  • He graduated from Howard University with Bachelor of Fine Arts in directing in year 2000.

  • Career timeline

    • 1993–2007: Theater, Deep Azure, and early television.

      • Boseman lived in Brooklyn, New York City, at the start of his career. In 2000, he was named a Drama League Directing Fellow.

    • 2008–2015: Breakthrough with 42 and Get on Up.
    • 2016–2019: Marvel Cinematic Universe, Marshall and 21 Bridges.

      • In 2016, Boseman began portraying the Marvel Comics character T'Challa / Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

    • 2020: Da 5 Bloods and Ma Rainey's Black Bottom.

      • In 2019 Boseman was announced as part of the cast for the Netflix films Da 5 Bloods, directed by Spike Lee, and Ma Rainey's Black Bottom, directed by George C. Wolfe.

  • Boseman was diagnosed with stage III colon cancer in 2016, which eventually progressed to stage IV before 2020. Boseman died at his home as a result of complications related to colon cancer on August 28, 2020, with his wife and family by his side.
  • A public memorial service was held on September 4, 2020, in Anderson, South Carolina, where the speakers included Boseman's childhood pastor as well as Deanna Brown-Thomas, daughter of James Brown, whom Boseman portrayed in Get on Up.
